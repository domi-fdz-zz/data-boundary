Data Boundary

Preliminary Data-Use Review · U.S. privacy, consent, source terms, and use constraints

Facts

Describe the proposed data use

Describe what the data contains, how it was obtained, whether it is identifiable or sensitive, where the data subjects are located, and how you plan to use it for research or commercialization. Once the facts are ready, start a preliminary assessment.

Need help organizing the facts? Ask the Fact Review panel on the right. Assessment usually takes 30-120 seconds.

Fact Review

Before assessment: add details here and ask for help organizing the relevant facts. This panel does not make a conclusion that the proposed use is permitted or not permitted.

After assessment: the preliminary assessment will load here. You may ask follow-up questions about the rationale, such as “why is the commercial use not recommended on the current facts?” This panel does not alter the rule decision; if a question changes a key fact, it will direct you back to the left-side facts for correction and reassessment.

Model Service Settings

Used to extract structured facts from the scenario and assist source-by-source review. Keys are stored only in the local user config on this Mac and are not bundled into the app package.

Instructions

Use this workspace to organize proposed data-use facts and generate a preliminary assessment based on the facts you confirm.

1. Describe the factsEnter what the data contains, where it came from, whether people can be identified, subject locations, source terms, and the intended use.
2. Confirm extracted factsReview the structured fields generated from your text. Correct uncertain fields before running the assessment.
3. Read the assessmentThe result separates the preliminary conclusion, open facts that may change it, and the sources checked for the assessment.
4. Use Fact ReviewAsk for help organizing facts before assessment, or ask follow-up questions about the rationale after assessment.
Example: A California biotech company plans to use a registered public gene-expression dataset with disease labels to train a commercial oncology model; the license and consent terms are unclear, and subjects may include California and Washington residents.
